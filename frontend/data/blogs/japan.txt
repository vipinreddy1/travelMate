14-Day Grand Japan Itinerary: Cities, Alps, and Coast
Day 1: Tokyo Arrival & Shinjuku

Morning/Afternoon: Arrive at Narita (NRT) or Haneda (HND). Activate the JR Pass. Take the Narita Express (N'EX) or Monorail into central Tokyo.

Evening: Check into a Shinjuku hotel. Recover from jet lag with a low-key dinner at a local ramen shop and a walk through the neon-lit Kabukicho district.

Day 2: Tokyo - Tradition & Tech

Morning: Visit Asakusa to see Senso-ji, Tokyo's oldest temple, and browse the Nakamise shopping street.

Afternoon: Take the subway to Akihabara. Explore multi-story electronics stores, anime shops, and grab lunch at a maid cafe or standing sushi bar.

Evening: Head to Shibuya to walk the famous Shibuya Crossing. Have dinner at a futuristic Izakaya.

Day 3: Tokyo - Youth Culture & Fashion

Morning: Walk through the serene Meiji Shrine forested grounds.

Afternoon: Explore Harajuku’s Takeshita Street for extreme youth fashion and crepe stands, then walk down Omotesando for high-end architecture.

Evening: Visit Roppongi for high-altitude city views at the Mori Tower observation deck, followed by dinner in the area.

Day 4: Hakone - Mt. Fuji & Hot Springs

Morning: Take the Romancecar train from Shinjuku to Hakone-Yumoto.

Afternoon: Do the "Hakone Loop"—ride the switchback mountain train, the cable car, the ropeway over volcanic sulfur vents (Owakudani), and a pirate ship across Lake Ashi for views of Mt. Fuji.

Evening: Check into a traditional Ryokan. Eat a multi-course Kaiseki dinner and soak in a hot spring (Onsen).

Day 5: Takayama - The Japanese Alps

Morning: Take the Shinkansen to Nagoya, then transfer to the Hida Wide View express train winding through the stunning river valleys of the Japanese Alps to Takayama.

Afternoon: Walk through Takayama’s beautifully preserved Sanmachi Suji historic district.

Evening: Have dinner featuring the region's famous melt-in-your-mouth Hida beef.

Day 6: Shirakawa-go & Kanazawa

Morning: Take a Nohi bus from Takayama to Shirakawa-go, a UNESCO World Heritage village famous for its traditional thatched-roof farmhouses.

Afternoon: Board another bus to Kanazawa. Visit Kenrokuen, ranked as one of Japan’s top three landscape gardens.

Evening: Walk through the Higashi Chaya geisha district. Eat fresh seafood from the nearby Sea of Japan.

Day 7: Kyoto - The Higashiyama District

Morning: Take the Thunderbird Express train from Kanazawa down to Kyoto. Drop bags at the hotel.

Afternoon: Visit Kiyomizu-dera, a massive wooden temple overlooking the city. Walk down the preserved historic streets of Sannen-zaka and Ninen-zaka.

Evening: Stroll through Gion, keeping an eye out for Geiko and Maiko (geishas). Dinner in the Pontocho alley overlooking the river.

Day 8: Kyoto - Silver Pavilion & Philosophers

Morning: Visit Ginkaku-ji (The Silver Pavilion) and its immaculate dry sand garden.

Afternoon: Walk the Philosopher’s Path, a scenic stone walkway along a cherry-tree-lined canal. End at Nanzen-ji temple to see its massive wooden gate and brick aqueduct.

Evening: Head to downtown Kyoto (Kawaramachi) for shopping and a casual dinner at Nishiki Market (if open late) or a nearby Tonkatsu restaurant.

Day 9: Kyoto - Bamboo & Golden Pavilion

Morning: Head west to Arashiyama very early to walk the Bamboo Grove before the crowds arrive. Visit Tenryu-ji temple.

Afternoon: Take the local bus to Kinkaku-ji (The Golden Pavilion), covered in real gold leaf.

Evening: Rest at the hotel. This is a heavy walking day; order takeout or eat at a simple noodle shop near Kyoto Station.

Day 10: Nara - Giant Buddhas & Sacred Deer

Morning: Take a 45-minute commuter train trip to Nara. Walk through Nara Park, where hundreds of sacred, free-roaming deer will bow for deer crackers.

Afternoon: Visit Todai-ji, a massive wooden building housing a 15-meter-tall bronze Buddha statue. Visit the lantern-lined Kasuga Taisha shrine.

Evening: Return to Kyoto to collect your bags, take a 15-minute Shinkansen to Osaka, and check into an Osaka hotel.

Day 11: Osaka - Street Food Capital

Morning: Visit Osaka Castle and explore the surrounding park.

Afternoon: Walk through the Shinsekai district, a retro entertainment area. Try Kushikatsu (deep-fried skewered meats and vegetables).

Evening: Hit Dotonbori. This is the neon-drenched epicenter of Osaka’s food culture. Eat Takoyaki (octopus balls) and Okonomiyaki (savory pancakes).

Day 12: Himeji & Hiroshima

Morning: Take the Shinkansen west. Stop halfway at Himeji Station. Walk down the street to tour Himeji Castle, Japan’s most spectacular and best-preserved original samurai castle.

Afternoon: Get back on the Shinkansen to Hiroshima. Check into the hotel.

Evening: Eat Hiroshima-style Okonomiyaki (layered with noodles) for dinner.

Day 13: Hiroshima & Miyajima Island

Morning: Visit the Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park, the A-Bomb Dome, and the Peace Memorial Museum. It is a sobering but essential historical experience.

Afternoon: Take a local train and the JR Ferry to Miyajima Island. See the Itsukushima Shrine and its famous "floating" vermilion Torii gate.

Evening: Take the Shinkansen all the way back to Tokyo (approx. 4 hours). Check into a hotel near Tokyo Station or Shinagawa for easy airport access the next day.

Day 14: Tokyo - Final Shopping & Departure

Morning: Optional early morning visit to the outer Tsukiji market for a fresh sushi breakfast. Do last-minute souvenir shopping in Ginza or Tokyo Station's Character Street.

Afternoon: Take the Narita Express or Monorail to the airport.

Evening: Flight departs for Phoenix/Tempe.
